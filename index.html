--- layout: default title: Matthew Henry ---

PhD Candidate in the Department of Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences at McGill University, working with Prof. Tim Merlis.

I received a B.Sc. degree in mathematics from McGill University and an M.Sc. degree in atmospheric and oceanic sciences and mathematical modelling in Paris. I also did a small internship in natural language processing in Grenoble. To learn more, you can view my CV here.

The main goal of my research is to improve our understanding of the horizontal and vertical structure of atmospheric warming as greenhouse gas concentrations increase. I mainly use idealized models of the climate system, which by sacrificing precision, improve our understanding of the complex climate system.

Manuscripts:

  • Henry, M. and T.M. Merlis: The role of the nonlinearity of the Stefan-Boltzmann law on the structure of radiatively forced temperature change. Journal of Climate, 2018. doi:10.1175/JCLI-D-17-0603.1. Paper and poster.
  • Merlis, T.M. and M. Henry: Simple estimates of polar amplification in moist diffusive energy balance models. Journal of Climate, 2018. doi:10.1175/JCLI-D-17-0578.1. Paper.
